Research

Physics-aware intelligence for space systems.

CIRO connects astrodynamics, optimal control, scientific machine learning, and robotics to address estimation, autonomy, mission design, and sustainability.

01 · Space domain awareness

Physics-informed orbit determination

Physics-informed neural methods combine sparse angular observations with orbital dynamics for accurate initial orbit determination, maneuver detection, and uncertainty-aware tracking in GEO, X-GEO, and cislunar regimes.

Our approach integrates governing equations and mission constraints directly into learning and optimization, improving data efficiency, physical consistency, and confidence in the resulting decisions.

02 · Trustworthy autonomy

Safe reinforcement learning

Learning-based controllers are paired with stability analysis and certification tools so autonomous aerospace systems can remain robust, interpretable, and safe.

Our approach integrates governing equations and mission constraints directly into learning and optimization, improving data efficiency, physical consistency, and confidence in the resulting decisions.

03 · Optimal guidance and control

Physics-informed trajectory optimization

Pontryagin Neural Networks and the Theory of Functional Connections provide fast, continuous solutions to constrained optimal-control and mission-design problems.

Our approach integrates governing equations and mission constraints directly into learning and optimization, improving data efficiency, physical consistency, and confidence in the resulting decisions.

04 · Planetary robotics

Autonomous lunar and planetary landing

Image-based guidance, hazard avoidance, and reinforcement meta-learning support precise landing in uncertain and previously unseen environments.

Our approach integrates governing equations and mission constraints directly into learning and optimization, improving data efficiency, physical consistency, and confidence in the resulting decisions.

05 · Small-body missions

Asteroid proximity operations

Optimization, learning, and adaptive guidance enable mapping, hovering, landing, inspection, and close-proximity maneuvers around irregular small bodies.

Our approach integrates governing equations and mission constraints directly into learning and optimization, improving data efficiency, physical consistency, and confidence in the resulting decisions.

06 · Space-object characterization

Light-curve intelligence

Physics-informed and transformer-based methods estimate spacecraft attitude, angular velocity, shape, and behavior from photometric observations.

Our approach integrates governing equations and mission constraints directly into learning and optimization, improving data efficiency, physical consistency, and confidence in the resulting decisions.
