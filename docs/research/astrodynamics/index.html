Physics-informed orbit determination

The increasing number of artificial objects in the X-GEO region creates a new challenge for space situational awareness. Physics-Informed Orbit Determination (PIOD) combines single-layer neural networks trained via Extreme Learning Machine with a high-fidelity orbital model including non-spherical Earth gravity, third-body perturbations, and solar-radiation pressure.

PIOD achieves arcsecond-level accuracy without requiring an initial orbital guess or a priori orbital information. Applications include the Falcon 9 rocket body, the Peregrine spacecraft, and Peregrine impact-point prediction.

Attitude determination through light-curve analyses

Photometric light curves contain rich information for estimating the attitude of space objects, but the inverse problem is difficult. CIRO investigates bidirectional LSTM recurrent networks and convolutional Deep Operator Networks trained on high-fidelity synthetic light curves for geostationary satellites.

Both architectures achieve mean attitude errors below one degree. The operator-learning formulation can also estimate satellite states at times where no brightness measurements are available, bridging observational gaps in space situational awareness.

Space-environment evolution and orbital capacity

Multishell, multispecies source–sink models represent active, slotted, unslotted, and derelict satellites, rocket bodies, and trackable and untrackable debris while accounting for drag, collisions, explosions, and post-mission disposal.

These models support long-term population analysis and orbital-capacity assessment: the practical and physical limit on how many operational objects an orbital regime can safely, reliably, and sustainably support.

Asteroid mapping, cislunar surveillance, and interplanetary mission design

CIRO studies global mapping of irregular asteroids using frozen orbits and out-of-nadir pointing, optimizes 2D Lattice Flower Constellations for full-sky and cislunar surveillance, and designs interplanetary trajectories with unpowered and powered flybys and deep-space maneuvers.

Representative applications include asteroid 2002 AT4 sample return and trajectories toward Uranus and Neptune.
