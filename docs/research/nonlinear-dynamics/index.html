Identification of nonlinear dynamics in aerospace.

Interpretable models and formal stability certificates for learning-enabled aerospace control.

Astrodynamics & space situational awareness →Optimal guidance & control →Identification of nonlinear dynamics →

Stability certification of reinforcement-learning agents

Reinforcement learning is a promising alternative to classical guidance and control, but the lack of formal stability guarantees limits its adoption in critical aerospace applications.

CIRO uses Sparse Identification of Nonlinear Dynamics (SINDy) to approximate closed-loop dynamics from trajectories generated by a trained RL agent. System states, the value function, its derivative, and its integral support analytic representations of both the dynamics and control policy.

The derived value function becomes a Lyapunov-function candidate, enabling stability certification while the analytic controller improves policy explainability and reliability. Demonstrations include optimal attitude control and hovering near an asteroid.
